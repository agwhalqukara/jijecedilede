Where is the winner of season five, Emma Dean?

Each and every contestant select their own path after coming out of ‘Master Chef Australia.’ And the same rule applies to the winner too. So, what is the chef Emma Dean doing now?

After five years of winning the show, the former town planner published her own cookbook, ‘A Homegrown Table’. As of now, ‘Master Chef Australia’ star Dean hosts Network Ten television series, ‘My Market Kitchen’.

And she hosts the show with 2013’s runner-up and her fellow co-host from the program, Lynton Tapp. Not to mention that Emma Dean show ‘My Market Kitchen’ is in its third season.

In an interview, while talking about her show Emma Dean said

“I don’t have a restaurant because I am a bit risk-averse,” “However, the show is really fun and the best part is I get to do it with my best mate!”

Other than that, she runs workshops on sustainable cooking around Victoria. Nevertheless, it seems that chef Emma Dean is going great with her show already in the third season. Let’s hope she does even better in the near future.

Emma Dean Wiki-Bio:

Australian cook and television presenter Emma Dean was born on October 16, 1977, in  Victoria, Australia. Currently, Emma Dean age is 40.

With her parents, Dean started cooking from the very young age. She also has an elder sister named Rachel Dean who works as a clothing designer.

Dean completed her studies at Monash University and RMIT(Royal Melbourne School Institute of Technology) and later on received a  master’s degree in environment and planning.
